Pricing of electricity in Austria

2026-04-15

Why do you pay 25 cents per kilowatt, despite signing contract for 12? How much dotations do you pay for green energy? What all the abbreviations in the yearly bill mean?

The way how electricity price is defined and charged is quite standard for Europe but may be difficult to understand for immigrants. Complicated combination of taxes and charges makes final payment quite different from what is advertised by energy providers.

General principles

As the most of house-related services, electricity is paid based on predicted average with yearly settlement according to actual consumption. This means, provider assumes your consumption to be same as previous year; for the first contract it's predicted based on family size, size of the apartment etc. Based on this, yearly price is calculated and you pay 1/12th every month. Same as heating, it's same amount every month regardless of seasonal deviations.

Then after a year they check real consumption, calculate resulting cost and you either pay the difference or get it reimbursed. "A year" is not necessarily a calendar year, in my current contract they count 1st April till 31st March.

Most providers allow to adjust monthly payments in some range. In the end, of course, you pay the same in total.

This may seem a bit strange to people coming from Ukraine like me, that instead of paying for real consumption you delay it till the next year. But this allows to cut the seasonal spikes for households and also avoids hassle with constantly changing monthly payments. Also for freaks like me it's much simpler to check the accounting once a year instead of every month.

Providers

According to Austrian law, providers who sell electricity are separated from network that delivers it. Law guarantees free and simple choice of provider, whereas providers are competing with freely defined prices. But there is a single network provider in every area (who supports physical network), their price is defined by the government.

You can check and precalculate current prices on E-Control or Durchblicker. There you can as well change to another provider in just couple of clicks. It’s really guaranteed by the law to be that simple to terminate or switch provider.

Just be careful, check first your binding period. In Austria it's usual to have contracts with binding period. People here like stability.

My hint: couple of months before the end of binding period check with your provider directly. Most providers can offer additional loyalty discount with the next binding period.

This also works with everything else (like internet, mobile). No sense in staying "unbound" after the end of binding period, it's cheaper to have new binding. Or found a provider without binding from the beginning, if it's important for you.

Price structure

I created a small utility that calculates the price for you with all the details.

Price is split to fixed part (Grundpreis) and consumption part (price of kWh). In my contract I have 14.3 cents per kWh plus 3.99 EUR monthly. But I end up paying 26 cents per kWh plus 123 EUR. Let’s see why.

All numbers below are for 2026. Taxes and fees slightly change every year.

First category is obvious: these are costs to the provider, direct per consumption and monthly.

Then are network costs. The fixed price for the network is called "Leistungspreis", meaning "price of power". It depends on maximum power of your connection, normally it's "level 7". For Vienna it's 54 EUR per year (4.8 a month). As you can see, there are 3 options: without power measurement (normal for households), with measurement (price is defined based on the actual maximum power for the period) and with interrupting connection.

Grundpreis = (GP + NL + NV + ML )... Consumption = (CP + NN

GP - Grundpreis (fix price) ane CP - consumption price (per kWh) are defined in your contract with provider.

NL

- Netzwerk Leistungspreis - price per year for provided power by the network. Defined based on the maximum power provided, 54 EUR for regular house or apartment.

NN

- Netznutzungsentgelt - price per kWh delivered by the network. 6.98 ct/kWh.
